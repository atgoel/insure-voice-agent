• Insurance product master information  
• Product code mappings  
• UIN lifecycle management  
• Product activation and expiry  
• Riders and plan categories  
• Product metadata and marketing information

4. Business Flow 
Step 1 — Product Creation 
Product is created in: 
tblProducts 
Contains: 
• Product name  
• Product type  
• Rider details  
• Marketing data  
Step 2 — Regulatory Approval 
UIN details are stored in: 
M_PHUB_UINCHANGEPRODUCTS 
Contains: 
• UIN number  
• Activation date  
• Expiry date  
• Active status  
Step 3 — Product Activation 
btIsActive = 1 
Product becomes available for: 
• Sales  
• Policy issuance  
• Distribution  
Step 4 — Product Expiry / Withdrawal 
btIsActive = 0 
Product becomes inactive.

8. Sample Business Queries 
Fetch Active Insurance Products 
SELECT 
    p.vcProductTitle, 
    p.vcProductType, 
    p.VcPlanCategory, 
    u.vcUIN, 
    u.dtProductActivationDate, 
    u.dtProductExpiryDate 
FROM tblProducts p 
INNER JOIN M_PHUB_UINCHANGEPRODUCTS u 
ON p.vcNVESTProductCode = u.vcNvestProductCode 
WHERE u.btIsActive = 1 
AND u.btIsDeleted = 0; 
 
Fetch Products Expiring Soon 
SELECT 
    vcProductName, 
    vcUIN, 
    dtProductExpiryDate 
FROM M_PHUB_UINCHANGEPRODUCTS 
WHERE dtProductExpiryDate <= DATEADD(DAY,30,GETDATE()); 
 
Fetch Products By Category 
SELECT 
    vcProductTitle, 
    vcProductType, 
    VcPlanCategory 
FROM tblProducts 
WHERE VcPlanCategory = 'Protection';

Purpose of this Table 
Insurance companies in India use: 
UIN (Unique Identification Number) 
Issued by: 
• Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India  
Each approved insurance product gets: 
• UIN number  
• Approval lifecycle  
• Validity dates  
This table stores: 
• UIN mapping  
• Active/inactive status  
• Activation/expiry dates

• Unique record ID
